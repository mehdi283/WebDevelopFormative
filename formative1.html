A Regular Ramadan Day in My Life

Ramadan is distinguished as a month of self-discipline,
self-examination and loyalty, which makes it special. In
order to follow the fasting schedule, I had to completely
change my daily routine. I am interested to share what my
days often include during this holy month.


Early Morning Suhoor routine

In the morning I wake up really early; I wake up at 3AM in the morning and start
reciting the Quran till 4:30. It is a beautiful time in the morning for sincere reflection
and profound communion. Afterward, I have Suhoor and make certain to eat well so
my energy lasts throughout the day; After having Suhoor, I brush my teeth and pray Fajr. Then i
sleep till 5:30 AM.


University Time

At 6:30 AM, my alarm rings on again, indicating that it's time to prepare for university. I quickly get dressed,
take a shower, and get ready for the lengthy ride. I live in Ajman, thus I travel to Ras Al Khaimah, the location
of my university, Bath Spa University, using bus at 7:20 AM. I reach the university by 9:00 AM after a two-hour trip,
and courses begin at 9:30 AM. Since I have only three days a week at my university, my schedule is a little easier to
handle during Ramadan. At 12:30 PM, my courses are over, It's 2:00 PM when I finally reach home.
