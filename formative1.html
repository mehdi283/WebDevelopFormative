A Regular Ramadan Day in My Life

Ramadan is distinguished as a month of self-discipline,
self-examination and loyalty, which makes it special. In
order to follow the fasting schedule, I had to completely
change my daily routine. I am interested to share what my
days often include during this holy month.


Early Morning Suhoor routine

In the morning I wake up really early, I wake up at 3AM in the morning and start
reciting the Quran till 4:30. It is a beautiful time in the morning for sincere reflection
and profound communion. Afterward, I have Suhoor and make certain to eat well so
my energy lasts throughout the day; After having Suhoor, I brush my teeth and pray Fajr. Then i
sleep till 5:30 AM.


Getting ready for University

At 6:30 AM, I woke up, feeling a little drowsy but eager to get on with my day. After taking a brief shower,
I get ready for college. Since I live in Ajman, I travel to Ras Al Khaimah, the location of my university, Bath Spa
University, by using bus at 7:20 AM. I get there at 9:00 AM after a two-hour drive, right before my lessons start at
9:30 AM. On class days, I concentrate on studying and maximizing my lectures because I only attend university three days a week.

Returning Home & Resting

At 12:30 PM, my classes are over, and I begin to head home. I eventually reach
home around 2:00 PM, feeling grateful however exhausted. To get my energy back for the evening, I pray and then sleep.

Iftari and Studying Time

I woke up at 6:00 PM, shortly before Iftar, and that's the moment I've been waiting for from morning!
It's always an enjoyable time for me to break my fast with family and mouthwatering food. I study till
8:00 PM after eating, being conscious to finish assignments and revise everything. Then, in order to
completely immerse myself in the spiritual ambiance of Ramadan nights, I go to the mosque at 8pm for prayers.

The food is one of the finest parts of Iftar! Although I appreciate an extensive variety of foods, my favorites are:

  • Biryani: Spicy and flavorful, it’s always a treat!
  • Chicken Korma: Rich, creamy, and full of delicious spices.
  • Mutton Namkeen Pulao: A perfect balance of tender mutton and fragrant rice.
  • Samosas: Crispy, filled with spicy potatoes or minced meat.
  • Fruit Chaat: A refreshing mix of fruits with a hint of spice.
  • Dates and Rooh Afza: A classic way to break my fast!

End of The Day

Realizing that I'll be getting up again in a few hours to resume this cycle,
I'm ready to go sleep by 10:00 PM. The routine is difficult, but it is also extremely
worthwhile. It takes discipline to balance fasting, prayers, and university life, but
Ramadan serves as a reminder of the value of patience, faith, and awareness.


My days throughout this holy month are like this: they are full of dedication, thankfulness, and purpose.
